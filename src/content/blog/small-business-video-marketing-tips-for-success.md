As the saying goes, a picture is worth a thousand words. But what about a video? Video marketing is on the rise every year. After the pandemic, as businesses adapt, it’s clear that using promotional videos for business is more important than ever. But how do you create compelling video content that will really engage your audience? And how can you make sure that your video marketing efforts will actually result in measurable business success?

Alright, let’s set the stage. Video marketing isn’t just another buzzword; it’s a game-changer for small businesses like yours. It’s all about building meaningful connections, boosting brand awareness, and, ultimately, driving those conversions. So, if you still need to jump on the bandwagon for creating a marketing video, now’s the time! In this blog post, we’ll share five key video marketing tips to help take your small business to the next level.

### Video Content Strategy

Revitalise your content with a creative video content strategy by identifying what makes your business unique. Develop videos tailored to your target audience, spanning various types like humour, product demos, success stories, behind-the-scenes glimpses, and testimonials. Ensure your video content strikes a balance of engagement and humanity, resonating with your customers. Consider the timing and frequency of your videos, as consistent posting enhances audience loyalty and boosts search engine optimisation.

### Connect With Your Audience On Their Favorite Platforms

In small business video marketing, there are many places to share your videos. Choose where to post based on your goals and the kind of videos you make. For short videos, try TikTok, Instagram, or YouTube for longer ones. LinkedIn is good for B2B businesses, and Facebook Live works for older audiences. TikTok is great for younger people. Check who uses each platform and when they’re most active, and try different ones to see what works best.

But don’t just post and be done; talk with your audience. Make content that connects with them, showing realness and openness. Building a group around your videos makes your brand stronger and creates loyal customers excited to see what you do next.

### Repurpose Your Content

Give your content a new life! Instead of letting your video content go to waste, repurpose and reuse it. Cut longer videos into shorter clips, make gifs, transcribe videos to create blog posts, and explore other creative ways to make the most out of your existing content.

Sprinkle a little SEO magic to ensure your videos get the attention they deserve. Incorporate relevant keywords in your video titles, descriptions, and tags to improve your chances of showing up in search results. Remember, it’s not just about creating great content; it’s about making sure people can find it too!

### Boost Your Small Business Presence on Social Media

When creating a promotional video for [marketing your small business on social media](/digital-marketing/social-media/), focus on highlighting the benefits you offer customers, such as product quality, excellent service, and what sets you apart from the competition.

Choose the right ad type for your goals, considering platforms like YouTube, Instagram, LinkedIn, or Twitter. [Promote your video on Facebook](/blog/how-facebook-videos-can-help-to-promote-your-business/) — with over 2.7 billion active users, it’s one of the best ways to promote your small business. You can create a Facebook page for your business, upload your video, and boost it with Facebook’s advertising tools. Make your CTA’s clear. Tell your viewers exactly what you want them to do after watching your video ad. Keep it simple and specific.

### Outsource Your Video Production

If you don’t have the in-house equipment or knowledge to create professional promotional videos, [outsourcing your video production](/blog/pros-of-outsourcing-video-production-for-your-business/) might be the best solution. One of the biggest advantages is that you don’t need to purchase expensive equipment or have a professional on staff. In fact, outsourcing can save you both time and money. When you outsource your video production, you employ skilled professionals who take the time to understand your message and deliver the product that meets your requirements. Outsourcing can also save on pre-production work and the complexities of post-production.

Your website is only as good as your web hosting service provider. The best providers should offer you competitive speeds, prompt and meaningful support, and advanced security. When looking for a Web Hosting Service, it's essential to figure out your needs and the goals you aim to achieve before settling on a service. We will go through the points you should think about to choose the right web hosting service for your business.

### How much support do you expect from your host?

The first thing you should do is estimate how much work you can do yourself and how much you want from your web hosting service. Some providers offer essential support over email, live chat, or phone but may take time to respond promptly. If you want a provider who can make changes to your site per your requests, then you may prefer a managed web hosting service provider who can use their knowledge and guidance to make the best decision for your requirements. If you need a quick fix, you need to know how fast your provider can deliver those results with little warning.

### Evaluate your expected traffic

Most hosting providers will charge based on the storage you need and how much bandwidth you use. The more users you expect to visit and interact with your site, the more bandwidth you need. If you know your general traffic levels, you can quickly gauge how much bandwidth you'll need with your provider. Work with your provider to work out the fairest cost for your website hosting.

Say you have a promotion on your website, and you generate way more traffic than what you're used to. Your bandwidth will struggle to keep up with everybody, and your site may slow down and even crash. It may be worth talking to your [website hosting services provider on the Northern Beaches](/web-development/web-hosting/) about potential traffic increases, so you can ensure your site can handle the workload.

### Know about the different hosting types

Web hosting services will offer different types of servers for different requirements. This is a quick breakdown of every server type and what they're suitable for:

- **Shared Hosting Server** – The most economical option for hosting low traffic websites
- **Managed Hosting Server** – Best for those who prefer to leave technical tasks to the experts
- **VPS Hosting Server** – If you have outgrown Shared Hosting, then this is the next step up
- **Cloud Hosting Server** – Best for rapidly growing websites that require scalable resources
- **Dedicated Hosting Server** – Large website hosting that gives you more control but is costly
- **Colocation Hosting Server** – The most expensive hosting option provides the power, bandwidth, IP addresses and cooling systems you require. You are expected to provide and look after everything else, including software, hardware and services. This is best for those who know what they're doing.

With rising competition, content creation using AI has become a game-changer. But do you just create content, or do you consider its format? Before starting, it’s crucial to decide whether short-form or long-form content suits your strategy, especially when you’re up to making [content for high-converting landing pages](/blog/five-copywriting-tips-increase-website-conversion/). Each format has a specific purpose and can impact your AI-driven search performance. Let’s explore how each format works and which one helps you rank higher.

### What Is Short-Form Content?

To define short-form content, it refers to brief, easily digestible pieces of content. While some suggest to check word count on pages to meet a specific length, the ideal word count for short-form content can vary depending on perspective—some consider 1,000 words short, while others set the threshold at 1,200 words. For videos, it’s typically 60 seconds or less. This format is perfect for quickly grabbing attention and delivering a concise message. - Social media posts
- Blog updates
- Emails
- Infographics
- Quick news articles

### How It Works for AI Search:

AI search algorithms, like Google’s RankBrain, value user engagement, and short-form content often performs well for specific, direct queries. This type of content can easily rank for long-tail keywords and short searches, offering quick answers to users’ needs.

### What Is Long-Form Content?

Long-form content goes in-depth on a topic, often exceeding 1,000 words or 60 seconds for videos. It’s perfect for providing detailed, informative content that educates your audience. If you’re looking to write long-form content, it’s crucial to incorporate relevant keywords and deliver real value. - In-depth blog posts
- How-to guides
- Ebooks and white papers
- Webinars
- Case studies

### How It Works for AI Search:

Long-form content tends to perform better in AI-driven search results because it offers more context, keywords, and opportunities to rank for multiple related terms. AI algorithms like those used by Google reward content that answers a broad set of questions and provides detailed, valuable insights. Longer content also tends to generate more backlinks, which further boosts SEO.

### Pros and Cons of Short-Form Content for AI Search

**Pros:**

- Ideal for quick answers, favoured by AI algorithms
- Mobile-friendly and easy to consume
- High social media share potential, boosting visibility

**Cons:**

- Limited depth, affecting rankings for complex queries
- Fewer backlinks
- Shorter shelf life compared to long-form content

### Pros and Cons of Long-Form Content for AI Search

**Pros:**

- Better for ranking high-volume, competitive keywords
- More comprehensive, covering a range of queries
- Increases dwell time, improving SEO
- More likely to earn backlinks

**Cons:**

- Requires more resources
- Can overwhelm users seeking quick answers
- May not drive immediate conversions

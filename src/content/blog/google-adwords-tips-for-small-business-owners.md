If you're a small business owner, you know that competing with the big guys can be challenging. But did you know that there are tools and strategies available to help level the playing field?

One such tool is Google Adwords. Adwords is a program that allows you to place ads on Google and its partner websites, including YouTube. You can choose where your ads appear and even precisely what they say.

Here are some tips for getting the most out of Adwords:

1. **Use keywords related to your business** in your ad text. This will help ensure that people searching on Google for terms related to your products or services will see an ad with your business name and contact info.

2. **If you offer a service, include a short testimonial** from a satisfied customer in your ad text. This will reassure potential customers that they will get quality service when they choose your business over others in the area.

3. **Include your location in your ads** so people can find you. For instance, you can use ad extensions to include a link with driving directions. Adding your location can also help Google bring in local campaigns and audiences.

4. **Use negative keywords,** so you aren't charged for people who aren't looking for your business specifically. Not all of the words used in searches related to what you offer will be positive or even neutral terms. If someone is searching for a competitor, you'll want to include negative keywords, so you aren't charged for the ad impression.

5. **Monitor your ads frequently** so that if there is a problem with them, you can resolve it right away. For instance, if one of your ads is displayed on an inappropriate site or gets filtered out by Google, you won't spend money on it.

6. **Try different Adwords campaign types** to see how they work with your business objectives and profit margins. There is Standard, which works best for most small businesses; Pay-Per-Click (PPC) online advertising, which lets you pay only when someone clicks on your ad; and Enhanced CPC, which gives you more control over where your ads appear.

7. If Adwords isn't producing results for you, **try different strategies** until you find what works best. Be sure to track everything so that later it can be determined if a particular campaign is working better for any reason. It may also be worth investing in [AdWords consulting](/blog/google-ppc-is-more-important-than-ever-for-your-business/) so you can find the best solution quickly.

8. **Increase your budget** after a while if you find that your ads are successful. This will help you reach even more people who might be interested in what you have to offer.

Google Adwords marketing can be an excellent tool for small business owners who want to increase their visibility and grow their business. With a little bit of research and effort, you can create ads that will help you stand out from the competition and reach your target market.

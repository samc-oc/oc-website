Ever wonder how some businesses always pop up first when you search online? It’s often because they use a powerful combo: SEO and Google Ads. If you run a local business, especially in places like the Northern Beaches, getting seen online is crucial. Let’s break down why these two tools, used together, are your secret weapon.

### What is SEO? Your Long-Term Online Friend

Think of SEO (Search Engine Optimisation) as building a strong foundation for your online house. It’s all about making your website truly excellent, so Google *wants* to show it to people. When you search for something, Google looks for the best, most relevant websites, and SEO helps your site be one of those top picks.

For local businesses, this means making sure Google knows exactly where you are and what you do. Key elements include:

- **Google My Business:** Having a super-complete and updated profile here is HUGE. It helps you show up in local map searches.
- **Good reviews:** People trust reviews, and so does Google.
- **Website content:** Make sure your website has clear information about your services, especially for your local area.

When your SEO is optimised, you’ll see a natural rise in visitors through free Google searches. This is how you increase organic traffic – more website visits without paying per click. This consistent flow of potential customers is a key factor in SEO for business growth.

As more people discover your business online through these efforts, [SEO increases brand awareness](/blog/boost-brand-awareness-with-seo/). Ultimately, to truly expand their reach and customer base, many businesses depend on this powerful strategy for sustainable online success.

### What are Google Ads? Your Instant Online Boost

Now, imagine you want to get noticed RIGHT NOW. That’s where Google Ads (also known as PPC, or Pay-Per-Click) comes in. It’s like buying a billboard on the busiest street in town, but online. When someone searches on Google, you can pay to have your ad appear at the top or bottom of the results.

Here’s how it works: you choose keywords (like ‘plumber Manly’ or ‘best coffee Northern Beaches’), and your ad can appear if someone searches for those words. You only pay when someone clicks on your ad, which is why it’s called pay-per-click marketing (PPC).

The best part? Google Ads are super targeted. You can tell Google to only show your ads to people in a specific area, like the Northern Beaches. This means your ads are seen by people who are looking for exactly what you offer, right in your neighbourhood. It’s a fantastic way to quickly boost your business and get immediate leads.

### Why They’re Better Together: The Ultimate Combo

You might think, “Why do both? If I do Google Ads, I get quick results. If I do SEO, I get free results.” But here’s the kicker: they make each other stronger!

1. **Double the Space, Double the Trust:** It’s powerful when someone searches and sees your business both in the paid ads (Google Ads) and the free results (SEO). It shows you’re a big player, trustworthy, and very relevant. This “doubling up” makes people more likely to click on *your* listing.
2. **Smart Learning:** What you learn from your Google Ads can help your SEO. For example, if a particular keyword brings in a lot of customers through your paid ads, you know that keyword is important. You can then make sure your website is optimised for that keyword for your SEO efforts.
3. **Filling the Gaps:** SEO builds long-term visibility, but it takes time. Google Ads can bring in customers *right now* while your SEO efforts are still brewing. So, you’re never waiting around for leads.
4. **Cost-Effective in the Long Run:** Good SEO can actually make your Google Ads cheaper! Google likes good, relevant websites. If your website is well-optimised (thanks to SEO), your ads often perform better and cost less per click.

Imagine you’re a local bakery. A [Northern Beaches Google Ads consultant](/digital-marketing/google-ads/) could set up ads for “birthday cakes fresh Northern Beaches” to get immediate orders. At the same time, an [SEO specialist in the Northern Beaches](/digital-marketing/seo/) would work on your website so that if someone searches for the “best bakery for sourdough bread in Northern Beaches,” your organic listing will pop up. You’re catching both the urgent customers and those doing their research!
